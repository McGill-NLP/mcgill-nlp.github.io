About STAMINA

Digital Platforms and the AI agents that operate on them (and through us as knowledge sources and companions) are beginning to shape large-scale features of society. The dual-use argument suggests that these powerful technologies can serve in solutions to the emerging epistemic and social crises they foment. Building a community of researchers and developers working towards such positive-outcome features and applications is our collective goal.


The STAMINA Working Group brings together researchers and developers interested in the intersection of social simulations, AI agents/safety, and social interaction technologies aiming to support pro-social and pro-truth interactions between humans, between AI agents, and between humans and AI agents.


If you are interested in how digital platforms shape user behavior, how AI agents extend platform affordances, and/or how data-driven approaches can lead to better designs of agents and platforms and inform platform and AI governance, STAMINA provides a welcoming forum for interdisciplinary discussion and collaboration. We welcome participants from computer science, social and psychological sciences, policy, and related fields.

We hold a bimonthly seminar series to showcase the research of our members. We will adapt STAMINA's activity from surveying the research of our members to adding more tailored, interactive events as we better understand the needs, interests, and challenges faced by the STAMINA research community. We plan to organize an upcoming conference workshop.

The idea for this Working Group emerged from the LLM-based Social Simulation Workshop held in Montreal at the Conference on Language Modelling (COLM).
